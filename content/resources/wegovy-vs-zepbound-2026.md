# Wegovy vs. Zepbound: Comparing Your GLP-1 Options

If you have been exploring GLP-1 medications, chances are you have come across both Wegovy and Zepbound. They are two of the most talked-about options available, and it is completely natural to wonder how they stack up against each other. The reality is that both are legitimate, well-studied medications, and the best choice depends on your individual health profile, your insurance coverage, and the conversation you have with your doctor.

This guide is here to give you the information you need to walk into that conversation feeling informed and confident. We are not here to pick a winner. We are here to help you understand the differences so you and your healthcare provider can make the decision that is right for you.

## How They Work: The Science in Plain Language

Both Wegovy and Zepbound belong to a class of medications called GLP-1 receptor agonists, but they work in slightly different ways.

### Wegovy (Semaglutide)

Wegovy's active ingredient is semaglutide, which mimics a hormone called GLP-1 that your body naturally produces. GLP-1 plays a role in regulating appetite and blood sugar. By activating GLP-1 receptors, semaglutide helps your brain recognize fullness signals more effectively, which can lead to reduced appetite and, over time, changes in body weight.

Wegovy is manufactured by Novo Nordisk and was approved by the FDA specifically for chronic weight management.

### Zepbound (Tirzepatide)

Zepbound's active ingredient is tirzepatide, which takes a dual approach. It activates both GLP-1 receptors and GIP receptors. GIP is another hormone involved in metabolism and appetite regulation. This dual-receptor mechanism is what sets tirzepatide apart from single-receptor medications.

Zepbound is manufactured by Eli Lilly and also received FDA approval for chronic weight management.

## Dosing and Administration

Both medications are administered as once-weekly subcutaneous injections, meaning a small injection just under the skin, typically in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm.

### Wegovy Dosing Schedule

Wegovy uses a gradual dose-escalation approach. You start at a lower dose and increase over several months to give your body time to adjust:

- Month 1: 0.25 mg weekly
- Month 2: 0.5 mg weekly
- Month 3: 1 mg weekly
- Month 4: 1.7 mg weekly
- Month 5 and beyond: 2.4 mg weekly (maintenance dose)

### Zepbound Dosing Schedule

Zepbound also follows a dose-escalation pattern:

- Weeks 1-4: 2.5 mg weekly
- Weeks 5-8: 5 mg weekly
- Further increases to 7.5 mg, 10 mg, 12.5 mg, or 15 mg based on your doctor's guidance

Your doctor will determine the right maintenance dose based on how you respond and how well you tolerate each level. This is one of those areas where individual experience really varies, and having open communication with your provider matters a lot.

## Side Effects: What People Actually Report

Both medications share similar side effect profiles, which makes sense given that they work through related pathways. The most commonly reported side effects include:

- **Nausea** — often most noticeable during the dose-escalation phase and tends to improve over time
- **Diarrhea or constipation** — digestive changes are common as your body adjusts
- **Decreased appetite** — this is part of how the medications work, but it can feel unfamiliar at first
- **Fatigue** — some people notice tiredness, especially in the first few weeks
- **Injection site reactions** — mild redness or irritation at the injection site

The gradual dose-escalation schedule for both medications is specifically designed to minimize side effects by giving your body time to adjust. Most people find that side effects are most noticeable during the first few weeks and during dose increases, then settle down. Your doctor can also adjust the pace of your dose escalation if you need more time.

## Cost Considerations

Let's be honest: cost is one of the biggest factors in this decision for most people, and there is no reason to feel uncomfortable about that.

### List Price

Both medications have high list prices, generally in the range of $1,000 or more per month before insurance or savings programs. However, very few people actually pay list price.

### Insurance Coverage

Coverage varies significantly between insurance plans. Some plans cover Wegovy but not Zepbound, or vice versa. Some cover both, and some cover neither. Your insurance formulary — the list of medications your plan covers — is the first place to check.

### Savings Programs

Both manufacturers offer savings programs for eligible patients with commercial insurance. Novo Nordisk offers a savings card for Wegovy that can bring costs down significantly. You can learn exactly how to access it in our [Wegovy savings card guide](/resources/wegovy-savings-card-guide). Eli Lilly offers a similar savings program for Zepbound.

When you are comparing costs, it is worth checking both manufacturer savings programs and asking your pharmacy to run both options through your insurance so you can see your actual out-of-pocket cost for each.

## Availability

Both medications have experienced periods of high demand, which has occasionally led to supply constraints for certain dose strengths. Availability can vary by pharmacy and by region. Your pharmacist can help you understand current stock for both medications, and some people find it helpful to check availability at multiple pharmacies.

## Which One Is Right for You?

This is the part where we want to be really clear: the right medication for you is a decision that belongs to you and your doctor. Here are some factors that typically come into play:

- **Your health history and other conditions.** Your doctor will consider your full medical picture, including any other conditions you manage.
- **Insurance coverage and cost.** For many people, the medication that is best covered by their insurance becomes the practical first choice.
- **How you respond.** Some people do better on one medication than another, and that is perfectly normal. It does not mean anything went wrong — bodies are just different.
- **Your doctor's experience and recommendation.** Healthcare providers who prescribe these medications regularly often have insights into what tends to work well for different patient profiles.

## Having the Conversation

The best thing you can do is go into your doctor's appointment with a basic understanding of both options and a willingness to discuss what matters most to you. You do not need to have all the answers beforehand. Your doctor is there to help you weigh the options.

Here are a few questions worth bringing up:

- Which of these medications does my insurance cover?
- Based on my health history, do you have a recommendation?
- What should I expect during the adjustment period?
- How will we decide on the right maintenance dose?
- What should I do if I experience side effects?

## The Bottom Line

Wegovy and Zepbound are both well-studied, FDA-approved options for chronic weight management. They work through related but distinct mechanisms, share similar side effect profiles, and both offer savings programs to help with cost. The differences between them are real but nuanced, and the right choice depends on your individual circumstances.

You are already doing something meaningful by taking the time to learn about your options. That curiosity and initiative is going to serve you well no matter which path you and your doctor choose.
